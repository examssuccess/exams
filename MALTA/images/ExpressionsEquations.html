$ (a.)\;\; x^2 - 49 \\[3ex] (b.)\;\; x^2 + 2x - 35 \\[3ex] $ (ii.) Hence, simplify the expression $\dfrac{x^2 - 49}{x^2 + 2x - 35}$

(b.) Rearrange the formula shown below to make m the subject.

$ s = k - m^2 \\[3ex] $

$ (a.) (i.) \\[3ex] (a.) \\[3ex] x^2 - 49 \\[3ex] = x^2 - 7^2 \\[3ex] = (x + 7)(x - 7)...\text{Difference of Two Squares} \\[5ex] (b.) \\[3ex] x^2 + 2x - 35 \\[3ex] = (x + 7)(x - 5) \\[5ex] (ii.) \\[3ex] \dfrac{x^2 - 49}{x^2 + 2x - 35} \\[5ex] = \dfrac{(x + 7)(x - 7)}{(x + 7)(x - 5)} \\[5ex] = \dfrac{x - 7}{x - 5} \\[5ex] (b.) \\[3ex] s = k - m^2 \\[3ex] \text{Add m² to both sides} \\[3ex] s + m^2 = k - m^2 + m^2 \\[3ex] s + m^2 = k \\[3ex] \text{Subtract s from both sides} \\[3ex] s + m^2 - s = k - s \\[3ex] m^2 = k - s \\[3ex] \text{Take the square root of both sides} \\[3ex] \sqrt{m^2} = \pm \sqrt{k - s} \\[3ex] m = \pm \sqrt{k - s} $
